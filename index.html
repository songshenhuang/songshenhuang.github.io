1. Introduction¶

Buying a house is a very important thing in our life. The dream of most workers is to buy their own house. However, based on the different requirements of each person, the price of the house is also different. In this project, I want to analyze the Ames, Iowa house prices after 2000. So that people can get a sense of what kind of house can they buy with their own fund based on this analysis. So I chose some factors that I think are very important to analyze housing prices. as well as the relationship between year of construction, living area, number of full bedrooms, and size of garage in car capacity.

3. Data Collection¶

All the analysis of the data from the kaggle website: https://www.kaggle.com/c/house-prices-advanced-regression-techniques/overview. The two main datasets are train.csv and test.csv. The first step is to merge the two files together and then pull out the data I need. Because then we're going to look at the most recent housing prices so we're going to sift through the data after the year 2000.

Data fields:¶

  • SalePrice: the property's sale price in dollars.
  • YearBuilt: Original construction date
  • GrLivArea: Above grade (ground) living area square feet.
  • FullBath: Full bathrooms above grade.
  • GarageCars: Size of garage in car capacity.

5. Hypothesis testing and machine learning¶

In [10]:
price_data = df[['SalePrice', 'GrLivArea']]
dataMatrix = np.matrix(price_data)
X, Y = dataMatrix[:,0], dataMatrix[:,1]
model = LinearRegression().fit(X,Y)
m = model.coef_[0]
c = model.intercept_
x_1 = np.linspace(X.max(), X.min())
y_1 = (x_1*m)+c
plt.plot(data['SalePrice'],data['GrLivArea'],'o')
plt.xlabel('Sale Price')
plt.ylabel('Area')

6.Coclusion¶

Through this tutorial, we analyze the relationship between houses price and sizes, year of construction, the number of Full Bathrooms and the capacity of garages. According to the data analysis and visualization, the area, year of construction, garage capacit, and the number of full bedrooms are related to the houses price. In addition, there is a big difference in the price between different garage sizes. We can see that houses that can park two cars sell best. In addition, from the number of different Full Bathrooms, prices of 3 Full Bathrooms are higher. Does this mean that houses from the 3 Full Bathrooms are more suitable for modern people? I used a linear regression model to predict house prices. And we can use this equation to give you a better idea of how big a house you can buy with your money. Or in order to buy the ideal house need to prepare how much fund.
